Life at APC

Celebrations, Competitions, and Connections

JANUARY 14

Uttarayan (Makar Sankranti)

The sun's transition into Capricorn. Celebrated with kite flying and charity (Dan-Punya), marking the onset of harvest season.

JANUARY 26

Republic Day

Flag hoisting ceremony and patriotic songs to honor the Indian Constitution and our nation's heroes.

MAGH SUD 5

Vasant Panchami & Shikshapatri Jayanti

Celebrating the arrival of spring and the gift of the Shikshapatri by Bhagwan Swaminarayan for the welfare of all souls.

MAGH VAD 14

Maha Shivratri

The 'Great Night of Shiva'. dedicated to the worship of Bhagwan Shiva with Bilva-patra puja, fasting, and Vedic chanting.

FEBRUARY

Sports Festival

A week-long athletic extravaganza. Cricket, Volleyball, and Chess tournaments to build teamwork and fitness.

FAGAN SUD 15

Holi & Fagan Punam

The festival of colors symbolizing the victory of good over evil (Prahlad & Holika), and the birthday of Bhagatji Maharaj.

CHAITRA SUD 9

Rama Navami & Swaminarayan Jayanti

A double celebration! Marking the birth of Maryada Purushottam Lord Rama at noon and Purna Purushottam Bhagwan Swaminarayan at night.

CHAITRA SUD 15

Hanuman Jayanti

Celebrating the ultimate devotee, Hanumanji. Students recite the Hanuman Chalisa collectively to gain strength and devotion.

MARCH / APRIL

Annual Function

A cultural showcase where students display talents in drama and music, and academic toppers are honored.

VAISHAKH SUD 5

Shastriji Maharaj Jayanti

Honoring the founder of BAPS, Brahmaswarup Shastriji Maharaj, who established the Akshar-Purushottam Upasana.

VAISHAKH VAD 12

Yogiji Maharaj Jayanti

Celebrating the prophet of youth activities! Special assemblies focused on his vision of 'Samp, Suhradbhav, and Ekta'.

JUNE

Freshers' Welcome

A grand induction for new students. Ice-breaking sessions, games, and a warm welcome into the APC family.

ASHADH SUD 2

Rath Yatra

The Chariot Festival of Lord Jagannath, Balbhadra, and Subhadra. Celebrated with a procession and special 'Mung-Bhat' prasad.

ASHADH SUD 15

Guru Purnima

A fundamental Sanatan festival honoring the Guru. Students perform pujan of the Guru, keeping the Guru-Shishya tradition alive.

SHRAVAN MONTH

Hindola Utsav

A month of artistic devotion where God is swung in ornate cradles (Hindolas) made by students from various creative materials.

SHRAVAN SUD 15

Raksha Bandhan

Celebrating the bond of protection. Students offer Rakhis to God, praying for protection from bad company and maya.

SHRAVAN VAD 8

Janmashtami

Birth of Lord Krishna. The hostel resonates with 'Nand Gher Anand Bhayo' through devotional singing and a midnight Aarti.

BHADARVA SUD 4

Ganesh Chaturthi

Welcoming Vighnaharta Ganeshji. A clay Murti is installed for ten days, with daily aarti and laddu prasad.

BHADARVA SUD 6

Mahant Swami Maharaj Jayanti

Celebrating the life of our Pragat Guru, H.H. Mahant Swami Maharaj, focusing on his virtues of humility and unity.

ASO MONTH

Navratri

Nine nights worshiping Shakti (Goddess Durga). We observe it through prolonged spiritual anusthans and devotion.

ASO SUD 10

Dussehra (Vijayadashami)

Celebrating the victory of Lord Rama over Ravana. A day to pledge victory over our inner vices.

ASO SUD 15

Sharad Purnima

Birth anniversary of Aksharbrahma Gunatitanand Swami. Celebrated under the full moon with 'Dudhpau' prasad.

ASO VAD 15

Diwali

The Festival of Lights. Lighting diyas to dispel darkness, symbolizing the awakening of inner knowledge.

KARTIK SUD 1

Govardhan Puja & Annakut

A grand offering of 1000+ vegetarian dishes to God, celebrating the New Year with joy, forgiveness, and brotherhood.

KARTIK SUD 11

Prabodhini Ekadashi

The day Bhagwan Swaminarayan received Diksha. It marks the end of Chaturmas and is observed with waterless fasting by many.

MAGSHAR SUD 8

Pramukh Swami Maharaj Jayanti

A global celebration of our eternal Guru H.H. Pramukh Swami Maharaj, whose life motto was 'In the joy of others, lies our own'.

DECEMBER 31

New Year's Eve Garden Party

Our signature year-end gala! An open-air party with live food, music, and brotherhood to welcome the Gregorian New Year with satsang values.
