Nourishment for Body and Soul

In the ancient Indian tradition, it is said that "Annam Brahma" — Food is Divine. At BAPS APC, we believe that the food our students consume plays a vital role in shaping not just their physical health, but their mental and spiritual well-being. Our Bhojanalay (Dining Hall) is not merely a cafeteria; it is a sacred space where nourishment is prepared with devotion and served with love. We strictly adhere to a pure vegetarian, Satvik diet, avoiding onions and garlic, which is known to promote clarity of thought and control over one's senses—essential attributes for a student.

The day begins with a wholesome breakfast that provides the energy needed for morning classes. Lunch and dinner are elaborate affairs featuring a rotating menu of traditional Gujarati and Indian cuisines, ensuring that students never tire of the options. From hot rotis and nutritious dals to seasonal vegetables and rice, every meal is balanced to provide the necessary proteins, vitamins, and minerals. Special attention is paid to the quality of ingredients; we source fresh, organic produce wherever possible to ensure the highest standards of health.

Feasts and Festivities

Life at BAPS APC is vibrant and joyful. To break the monotony of academic life, we host 12 Grand Feasts every year, typically coinciding with major festivals like Diwali, Janmashtami, and Annakut. On these days, the Bhojanalay transforms into a banquet hall, serving dozens of delicacies that give students a taste of home. The highlight of the year, however, is the exclusive Garden Party held on New Year's Eve. Set under the stars in our lush gardens, this gala dinner features live food stalls, music, and celebration, allowing students to ring in the new year with their hostel family in grand style.

Hygiene and Seva

Our state-of-the-art kitchen is equipped with modern cooking appliances to handle large volumes of food without successful hygiene. Cleanliness is our top priority, with strict protocols in place for food preparation and storage. However, the secret ingredient in our meals is 'Bhav' (devotion). The meals are often prepared and served by volunteers and Sadhus who see feeding the students as a form of service to God. This infusion of spiritual sentiment makes the food 'Prasad', sanctifying the body and mind of the receiver.

Dining at the APC is a communal experience. Students sit together, breaking bread across disciplines and years, fostering a strong sense of brotherhood. The discipline of eating together at fixed times instills a sense of routine and punctuality. Before every meal, a short prayer is recited, reminding us to be grateful for the sustenance we receive. This practice of mindfulness turns a simple act of eating into a spiritual ritual, grounding the students in gratitude and humility.
